Welcome to the municipality of Petah-Tikva

Petah Tikva also known as Em HaMoshavot ("Mother of the Moshavot"), is a city in the Central District of Israel, 10.6 km east of Tel Aviv. It was founded in 1878, mainly by Ultra-Orthodox Jews of the Old Yishuv, and became a permanent settlement in 1883 with the financial help of Baron Edmond de Rothschild. In 2019, the city had a population of 247,956. Its population density is approximately 6,277 inhabitants per square kilometre. Its jurisdiction covers 35,868 dunams. Petah Tikva is part of the Tel Aviv Metropolitan Area.
